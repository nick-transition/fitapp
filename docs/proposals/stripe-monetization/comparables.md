# Comparable Projects Research

## Overview

Competitive landscape analysis for FitApp's monetization strategy, covering fitness platforms, AI coaching products, open source fitness tools, and emerging Web3 protocol economies.

---

## 1. Strava

**Category:** Consumer fitness tracking (iOS/Android/Web)

**Monetization:** Freemium subscription

- ~40% of revenue from Strava Summit (premium subscription) at ~$7.99/mo or $59.99/yr
- Remaining revenue from partnerships, API access tiers, and brand deals
- Free tier retains social/tracking features; premium unlocks analytics, route planning, training plans

**Relevance:** Demonstrates that a large fitness user base (100M+ registered) can sustain meaningful subscription revenue without paywalling core features. The 40% subscription mix benchmark is a useful anchor for FitApp's blended revenue projection.

---

## 2. Trainerize

**Category:** B2B coach platform (white-label)

**Monetization:** SaaS per-coach subscription

- Coaches pay $5–$25/mo per active client
- Platform handles workout delivery, messaging, progress tracking
- ABC Fitness (parent company) upsells billing and payment processing

**Relevance:** Validates the B2B coach layer as a distinct revenue stream. FitApp's Coach Revenue share (20% of protocol revenue) maps directly to this segment. Trainerize shows coaches will pay meaningfully for tools that reduce administrative overhead.

---

## 3. wger (Workout Manager)

**Category:** Open source fitness tracker

**License:** AGPL-3.0

**Scale:** ~35,000 active users, self-hosted deployments across gyms and research institutions

**Funding:** Donation-funded via Open Collective; no venture backing

**Key insight:** AGPL-3.0 forces SaaS forks to contribute back or pay for a commercial license. wger sustains itself on community goodwill alone — no structured contributor compensation.

**Relevance:** Direct comparable for FitApp's open source strategy. wger proves the market exists but also reveals the ceiling: without a contributor incentive layer, growth stalls. FitApp's Proof of Contribution protocol is the mechanism wger lacks.

---

## 4. Future.co

**Category:** AI + human coaching (consumer)

**Monetization:** High-touch subscription

- $199/mo for weekly check-ins with a certified personal trainer augmented by AI
- AI handles form analysis, plan generation; humans handle motivation and accountability
- ~2,500 coaches on platform (as of 2024)

**Relevance:** Establishes willingness-to-pay ceiling for the premium coaching segment. At $199/mo, even capturing a small slice of Future's addressable market represents significant ARPU. FitApp's AI-first, coach-optional model targets users priced out of Future.

---

## 5. Virtuals Protocol

**Category:** AI agent marketplace (Base / Ethereum L2)

**Monetization:** Token-gated agent deployment + revenue share

- Developers stake VIRTUAL tokens to deploy agents; protocol takes a fee on agent interactions
- Revenue flows back to token holders and contributing developers
- Built on Base; ~$400M TVL at peak (2024)

**Relevance:** Proof that a protocol economy around AI agents is viable at scale. FitApp's open source strategy (Drips-based streaming payments, Proof of Contribution) is conceptually downstream of Virtuals — applying the same primitives to fitness AI rather than general-purpose agents.

---

## 6. OpenAI GPT Store

**Category:** AI agent/tool marketplace (Web2)

**Monetization:** Creator revenue share

- OpenAI distributes a share of ChatGPT Plus subscription revenue to top GPT builders
- Opaque allocation formula (engagement-weighted); no public per-GPT rate card
- ~3M GPTs created since launch (Jan 2024)

**Relevance:** Validates consumer appetite for specialized AI "agents" built on a base model. Also a cautionary tale: opaque revenue allocation creates distrust. FitApp's on-chain contribution tracking addresses this directly — every payout is auditable.

## Key Takeaways for FitApp

1. **Subscription revenue at 40% mix is validated** — Strava's benchmark supports FitApp's blended projection
2. **B2B coach tools command real willingness-to-pay** — Trainerize and Future.co confirm the coach segment
3. **AGPL-3.0 is the right open source license** — wger proves the model; FitApp adds the missing incentive layer
4. **Protocol economies work at scale** — Virtuals shows the primitives; FitApp applies them to fitness
5. **Transparent contribution accounting is a differentiator** — GPT Store's opacity is the gap FitApp's on-chain tracking fills
